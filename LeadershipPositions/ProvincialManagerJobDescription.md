Provincial Manager Job Descriptions for Whence

Financial Services

Job Description: Provincial Manager
Department: Senior Operations &amp; Strategic Leadership
Reports To: Technical Director / Executive Committee (Top Management)
Direct Reports: District Managers (5+ districts), Specialized Unit Managers
Location: Provincial Headquarters / Regional Hub
Date: 2026

1. Job Purpose
The Provincial Manager serves as the Strategic Growth Driver &amp; Portfolio
Architect for the province/region. This role does not duplicate district or branch
management, but instead focuses on strategic oversight, provincial portfolio
health, cross-district synergy, market innovation, and long-term value
creation aligned with the $100M valuation target. The Provincial Manager ensures
provincial operations contribute predictably and sustainably to institutional growth while
managing systemic risks and opportunities.

2. Key Responsibilities

A. Strategic Portfolio &amp; Market Leadership
 Develop and execute provincial growth strategy aligned with the 5-year $100M
roadmap.
 Oversee provincial portfolio health across all districts, focusing on
systemic/market risk (risk inherent to the entire market) reduction and
diversification.
 Drive market-specific product innovation (e.g., agricultural loans, payroll
lending partnerships, etc).
 Identify and cultivate strategic provincial partnerships (large employers,
government bodies, industry associations).

B. High-Level Oversight &amp; Validation
 Validate district-level aggregated reporting for accuracy and consistency.
 Oversee provincial risk dashboard including:
o Long-term delinquency trends
o Cross-district default correlations
o Regulatory and market risk exposure
 Conduct quarterly deep-dive reviews of 1–2 districts (not branches, unless
otherwise) focusing on strategy execution.

C. Forward-Looking Strategic Supervision
 Focus on quarterly and annual horizon planning, ensuring districts are
preparing for future:
o Regulatory changes
o Market expansions
o Product launches
o Technological upgrades
 Monitor provincial economic indicators and adjust strategy accordingly.
 Ensure capital allocation and resource planning aligns with provincial growth
targets.

D. Inter-District Synergy &amp; Governance
 Facilitate best practice sharing across districts through provincial forums.
 Resolve inter-district conflicts regarding territory, resources, or strategy.
 Oversee provincial talent pipeline for senior roles.
 Ensure consistent policy application across all districts.

E. Strategic Initiative Leadership
 Lead provincial rollouts of new products, systems, or processes.
 Drive provincial marketing and branding initiatives.
 Represent the institution in provincial industry forums and regulatory
engagements.

F. Value Creation &amp; Valuation Contribution
 Ensure the province meets its annual valuation contribution target.

5. Supervision Guidelines: What NOT to Do
To prevent micromanagement and role confusion, Provincial Managers MUST NOT:
 Intervene in district-level operational decisions
 Directly manage branch staff or day-to-day branch operations
 Handle individual loan approvals or collection cases
 Duplicate district manager validation work
Instead, Provincial Managers should focus on:
 Strategic portfolio oversight (provincial aggregates, not individual branches)
 Systemic risk management (trends, correlations, market risks)
 Strategic resource allocation (capital, talent, technology)
 High-level stakeholder management (regulators, major partners)
 Forward-looking strategic planning (6–18-month horizon)
