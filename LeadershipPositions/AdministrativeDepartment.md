1. DEPARTMENT OVERVIEW &amp; STRATEGIC ALIGNMENT
The general purpose of administration in a company or corporate setting is to ensure
the efficient and effective operation of the organization by organizing, planning, and
coordinating resources, processes, and people to achieve business goals. It acts as the
backbone of the organization, providing the necessary structure to support daily tasks
and strategic objectives. 

Key purposes of administration include:
 Operational Efficiency: Streamlining daily workflows, managing office facilities,
maintaining records, and ensuring that necessary tools and supplies are
available.
 Coordination of Resources: Aligning human resources, technology, and
material resources to maximize productivity.
 Strategic Support: Implementing company policies, monitoring performance,
and providing data to support decision-making at higher management levels.
 Communication &amp; Interaction: Acting as a central point of contact for internal
staff and external stakeholders, ensuring smooth information flow.
 Compliance &amp; Risk Management: Ensuring the company adheres to legal,
regulatory, and reporting requirements. 

Core Functions of Administration
Administration generally involves five key functions often summarized as:
1) Planning: Defining objectives and strategies.

3) Staffing: Managing personnel needs and on-boarding.
4) Directing/Leading: Motivating employees and guiding efforts.

keep the business on track. 
Ultimately, administration transforms corporate strategy into daily action, providing the
stability required for growth. Therefore, the Administration Department at Whence

core administrative functions of Planning, Organizing, Staffing, Directing, and
Controlling, the department directly supports sustainable profit growth and institutional
scalability.
Under the leadership of the Manager Administration (MA), the department ensures
that all branches, support units, and strategic initiatives are coordinated, efficient, and
aligned with institutional growth targets.
The department is structured to fully cover the Five Core Functions of Administration
