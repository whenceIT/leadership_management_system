Recoveries Coordinator/Manager Job Description for

Whence Financial Services

Department: Recoveries &amp; Special Collections
Reports To: Head of Risk &amp; Collections / Technical Director
Direct Reports: Recovery Specialists (Cross-Branch, Escalated Accounts, Dormant
Accounts, Legal, Skip Tracing), Recovery Officers (LCs with dual roles)
Location: Remote or Headquarters (with nationwide coordination responsibility)
Date: 2026

1. Job Purpose
The Recoveries Coordinator/Manager is a Strategic Collections Leader responsible
for transitioning the Recoveries Department from a support function to a profit-
generating unit. Initially operating as a Recoveries Coordinator implementing
established recovery strategies while managing Loan Consultants with dual recovery
responsibilities, the role graduates to Recoveries Manager upon demonstrating ability
to generate sustainable income, optimize recovery processes, and achieve self-
funding operations. Seniority and reward are determined by revenue recovered and
net income generated, comparable to branch, district, and provincial managerial tiers.

3. Key Responsibilities

A. Recovery Strategy Implementation &amp; Optimization
 Execute the 5-parameter recovery framework (Runaway, Escalated, Dormant,
Legal, Skip Trace)
 Continuously optimize recovery strategies based on performance data
 Develop and implement graduated recovery tactics for each parameter
 Ensure proper system tagging and attribution for all recoveries

B. Team Management &amp; Development
 Manage dual-role Loan Consultants in recovery functions
 Develop specialist capabilities within the team
 Create and maintain recovery knowledge base and training materials
 Implement succession planning to prevent knowledge loss due to promotions

C. Income Generation &amp; Sustainability
 Direct Recovery Revenue: Maximize collections through 5 parameters
 Revenue Trading: Participate in branch/product revenue sharing
 Recovery Service Fees: Charge branches for specialized recovery services
 Legal Recovery Monetization: Optimize cost-to-recovery ratios
 Cross-Branch Commission: Manage 50/25/25 attribution model
 New Revenue Streams: Develop additional recovery-based income

D. Process &amp; System Management
 Ensure accurate data tagging in Loan Management System
 Implement recovery performance dashboards
 Develop and maintain recovery process documentation
 Optimize technology utilization for recovery efficiency

 Coordinate with branches, districts, and provinces on recovery cases
 Manage legal counsel relationships and court processes
 Facilitate inter-branch cooperation on runaway clients
 Report recovery performance to senior management

7. Path to Recoveries Manager Promotion

A. Promotion Requirements
1. Financial Sustainability:

 Department covers all operational costs for 6 consecutive months
 Net recovery income ≥ K100,000 monthly for 3 consecutive months
 Positive cost-to-recovery ratio maintained

2. Strategic Capability:

 Documented recovery strategy optimization
 Proven ability to innovate recovery processes
 Development of sustainable income streams

3. Team Development:

 Established specialist development program
 Knowledge retention systems implemented
 Succession planning for key roles

B. Benefits Upon Promotion
 Managerial salary and benefits
 Dedicated recovery office space
 Authority to hire dedicated recovery specialists
 Budget autonomy for recovery operations

 Participation in senior risk committee meetings

8. Team Stability &amp; Knowledge Retention Strategy

A. Dual-Role LC Management
 Structured Rotation: Prevent burnout while maintaining loan skills
 Career Pathing: Clear progression within recovery specialty
 Cross-Training: Ensure multiple team members understand each parameter
 Performance Recognition: Reward recovery excellence specifically

B. Knowledge Management System
 Recovery Case Database: Document all successful recovery approaches
 Parameter Playbooks: Detailed guides for each recovery type
 Training Certification: Formal recovery specialist certification
 Mentorship Program: Experienced recoveries staff mentor new members

C. Promotion Transition Protocol
 Mandatory Knowledge Transfer: 30-day transition period for promoted staff
 Shadowing Requirement: New staff shadow experienced specialists
 Documentation Update: Promoted staff must update process documents
 Continuity Planning: No single point of failure for any parameter
