1. JOB PURPOSE
The Manager Administration (MA) serves as the strategic orchestrator of
institutional enablement, leading the administrative function as the enterprise&#39;s
operational backbone. This role transforms administrative services from support
functions into strategic value centers that protect assets, optimize resources, govern
performance, mitigate risk, and directly enable the $100M valuation target.
The MA is a non-revenue-generating executive role responsible for the integrated
leadership of the General Operations, Performance Operations, and Policy &amp;
Training functions. Progression through Tiers 3, 2, and 1 is based on enterprise-
wide efficiency gains, risk mitigation scale, strategic enablement breadth, and
measurable contribution to institutional scalability and valuation resilience.

2. KEY RESPONSIBILITIES &amp; STRATEGIC CONTEXT

A. Integrated Administration Leadership
 Function Integration &amp; Synergy

 Orchestrate the seamless collaboration between GOM (operations), POM
(performance), and PTM (governance) functions
 Eliminate silos to create a unified administrative engine that anticipates
and serves institutional needs
 Chair the Administrative Leadership Council to align priorities and
resources

 Strategic Planning &amp; Resource Allocation

 Develop and execute the 3-Year Administrative Strategy aligned with
the $100M valuation roadmap
 Allocate the consolidated administrative budget across functions for
maximum ROI
 Lead enterprise-wide resource optimization initiatives (shared
services, centers of excellence)

B. Enterprise Efficiency &amp; Loss Prevention Oversight
 Cross-Functional Loss Prevention

 Oversee the integrated loss prevention framework spanning operations,
performance, and policy
 Ensure quantification methodologies are consistent and auditable across
all functions
 Report to executive leadership on total value preserved by the
administration function

 Institutional Productivity Governance

 Monitor the enterprise productivity index combining operational uptime,
staff performance, and policy compliance
 Identify systemic inefficiencies that span multiple functions and lead cross-
functional solutions
 Optimize the total cost of administration as percentage of revenue
(target: ≤8%)

C. Strategic Enablement &amp; Valuation Contribution
 $100M Valuation Enablement Leadership

 Map all administrative functions to specific valuation drivers and
milestones
 Ensure administrative capabilities scale ahead of business growth
requirements
 Lead administrative due diligence for expansion, mergers, or new
market entry
 Institutional Resilience Building

 Develop and maintain the business continuity and institutional
resilience framework
 Ensure crisis response protocols are integrated across operations,
performance, and policy
 Build administrative scalability models to support 2x, 5x, and 10x
growth scenarios

D. Talent &amp; Leadership Development
 Administrative Leadership Pipeline

 Develop succession plans for all managerial roles within the administration
function and/or the institution
 Mentor GOM, POM, and PTM through their tier progression pathways
 Build bench strength for key administrative competencies

 Cross-Functional Leadership Development

 Create rotation programs between administrative functions
 Develop enterprise administration competencies across the
organization
 Foster an enabling leadership mindset throughout the institution
